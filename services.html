What we offer

Care that fits your loved one's life — not the other way around.

Old Fashion Care coordinates certified, personally vetted professionals across five service areas. Every match is made by Regina based on your loved one's specific needs, preferences, and schedule. No call centers. No guesswork.

How OFC works

We coordinate the care. Certified professionals deliver it.

Old Fashion Care is a care coordination service. Regina vets, interviews, and personally selects certified self-employed care professionals — CNAs, PCAs, licensed cosmetologists, and certified pedicurists — and matches them to your family's needs.

This model gives you access to experienced, credentialed specialists without the overhead of a large agency. And because Regina manages the relationship personally, you always have a direct point of contact who knows your family's situation.

Daily assistance
01

Daily personal care — handled with dignity.

For many seniors, bathing, dressing, and personal hygiene become difficult long before they're ready to admit it. Our referred Certified Nursing Assistants (CNAs) and Personal Care Assistants (PCAs) provide hands-on daily support that keeps your loved one clean, comfortable, and confident — without making them feel like they've lost control of their own life.

What's included

  • Bathing and showering assistance
  • Dressing and grooming support
  • Oral hygiene
  • Toileting and incontinence care
  • Mobility assistance and fall prevention
  • Medication reminders

Provided by: Certified Nursing Assistants (CNAs) and Personal Care Assistants (PCAs) — credentialed, background-checked, and personally matched by Regina.

Ongoing
02

A clean, organized home makes everything easier.

When keeping up with the house becomes overwhelming, the effects go beyond clutter. A disorganized home creates fall risks, missed medications, forgotten appointments, and a quiet kind of stress that wears on quality of life. Our referred professionals handle the practical tasks so your loved one can focus on what matters.

What's included

  • Light housekeeping and tidying
  • Laundry and linen changes
  • Grocery shopping and errands
  • Medication reminders
  • Appointment scheduling and transportation coordination
  • Mail and correspondence management

Provided by: Personal Care Assistants (PCAs) — experienced in home management and daily living support.

Daily
03

Nutritious meals, prepared at home.

Skipped meals and poor nutrition are among the most common — and most overlooked — health risks for seniors living alone. Our referred professionals prepare fresh, balanced meals tailored to your loved one's dietary needs, restrictions, and personal preferences. No meal kits. No reheating. Real food, made at home.

What's included

  • Breakfast, lunch, and dinner preparation
  • Accommodation for dietary restrictions (diabetic, low-sodium, heart-healthy, etc.)
  • Grocery list planning
  • Kitchen cleanup after meals
  • Hydration reminders throughout the day

Provided by: Personal Care Assistants with meal preparation experience, matched to your loved one's specific dietary profile.

As scheduled
04

Looking good isn't a luxury. It's dignity.

When getting to a salon or barber becomes difficult, grooming often gets skipped entirely. That matters more than people realize — feeling well-groomed directly affects mood, confidence, and social engagement. Old Fashion Care coordinates licensed, certified grooming professionals who come to your loved one's home.

Hair care (licensed cosmetologist)

  • Haircuts and styling
  • Shampoo and conditioning
  • Color and treatment services

Foot care (certified traveling pedicurist)

  • Pedicures and nail care
  • Diabetic foot care and inspection
  • Manicures
A note on diabetic foot care: Foot health is a serious medical concern for diabetic seniors. Our referred pedicurist is certified in diabetic nail care and trained to flag concerns that warrant medical attention.

Provided by: A licensed Cosmetologist and a certified Traveling Pedicurist — both referred through OFC's vetted network.

Weekly
05

Connection is as important as care.

Isolation is one of the most serious health risks facing seniors today — linked directly to cognitive decline, depression, and physical deterioration. Companionship isn't a nice-to-have. It's part of the care. Our referred professionals provide supervised outings, social engagement, and genuine human connection on a regular basis.

What's included

  • Supervised community outings (restaurants, parks, errands, events)
  • Transportation to appointments and social activities
  • Engagement in hobbies and personal interests
  • Conversation and daily social interaction
  • Support for family visits and gatherings

Provided by: Personal Care Assistants matched not just by certification but by personality — Regina specifically considers compatibility for companionship matches.
