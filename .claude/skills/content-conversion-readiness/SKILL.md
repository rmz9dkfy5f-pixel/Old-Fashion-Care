# Content and Conversion Readiness

## Purpose
Make sure the website is not just technically working, but clear enough to convert visitors into leads, bookings, calls, or inquiries.

## Non-negotiable rules
- Do not invent business claims, credentials, pricing, reviews, guarantees, or service areas.
- Do not add fake urgency or manipulative claims.
- Do not sacrifice clarity for clever copy.
- Treat missing CTA clarity as a business risk.

## Audit surfaces
- Home page hero.
- Primary CTA path.
- Services/offers.
- Pricing or quote flow if present.
- Booking/contact page.
- About/trust section.
- Testimonials/reviews if present.
- Footer contact details.
- Mobile above-the-fold view.

## Checklist

### Clarity
- The visitor can tell what the business does within 5 seconds.
- The target customer is obvious.
- Primary service/offer is specific.
- Location/service area is clear when relevant.
- Page sections answer likely buyer questions.

### Conversion
- Primary CTA is visible above the fold.
- CTA wording is action-specific.
- CTA destination works.
- Phone/email/contact options are easy on mobile.
- Form friction is reasonable.

### Trust
- Real business details are present where appropriate.
- Photos are relevant and not misleading.
- Reviews/testimonials are real or omitted.
- Policies/expectations are clear for bookings.
- No placeholder content remains.

### Consistency
- Business name is consistent.
- Phone/address/hours are consistent.
- Tone is consistent.
- Service names are consistent across nav, page copy, metadata, and forms.
